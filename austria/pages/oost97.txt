== 1.Bundesliga


<!-- $table1$ - Table -->

[*] LASK and FC Linz are to merge into LASK Linz at the end of the season;
    because of that, Admira/Wacker will play the relegation playoff against
    Vorwärts Steyr.  Admira/Wacker themselves are to merge with VfB
    Mödling (into "VfB Admira/Wacker Mödling"); what happens with
    the place of Mödling in the second division is still unclear and
    depends on the result of the relegation playoff.


Note: FC Admira/Wacker changed name to SC Niederösterreich FC Admira/Wacker.
Grazer AK played most home matches in the Alpen-Stadion of Kapfenberg, while
their own Casino-Stadion was being renovated.  FC Linz and LASK Linz (new
name for Linzer ASK) played the first few matches of the season in the
Union-Stadion of Wels.  SCN FC Admira/Wacker played several matches in
Krems and Sankt Pölten in a vain effort to gain a new identity as the
club of the "Land" (province) Niederösterreich.

[Note: Vorwärts were given a Bundesliga license on appeal and have
 threatened to go to court in order to obtain a place in next season's
 1. Bundesliga, arguing that Admira/Wacker were 10th in the 1.BL and
 should have been relegated automatically, while they should have been
 given the 1st division place by default as their true opponents, FC Linz,
