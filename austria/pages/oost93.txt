AUSTRIA, Feeder Leagues of the Second Bundesliga and below.
-----------------------------------------------------------

The structure of the Austrian "football pyramid" is as follows:

     Erste Bundesliga (10 teams from 1993/94, 1 directly relegated,
                       nr.9 plays off with nr.2 of Zweite BL.
                       36 matches.)
            |
            |
            |

     Zweite Bundesliga (16 teams in 1993/94, 1 directly promoted, nr.2
                        plays off with nr.9 of Erste BL. 3 directly
                        relegated. 30 matches.)

            |
            |
           /|\
          / | \
         /  |  \
      OST MITTE WEST

OST
___
     Regionalliga Ost (16 teams, 1 directly promoted to Zweite BL,
                       number of relegated teams dependent on teams
                       relegated from Zweite BL.)
     Three feeder leagues:
     - Landesliga Niederösterreich (14 teams)
     - Landesliga Burgenland (16 teams)
     - Wiener Liga (16 teams)
     All these leagues have one team promoted to the Regionalliga Ost.

MITTE
-----
     No Regionalliga. Instead, three independent Landesliga's:
     - Landesliga Kärnten (16 teams)
     - Landesliga Oberösterreich (14 teams)
     - Landesliga Steiermark (16 teams)
     The champions of these three leagues play off for one spot in the
     Zweite BL. For all leagues, number of relegated teams depends on
     the winner of the playoff and the teams relegated from Zweite BL.

WEST
----
     The Regionalliga West is formed for Spring only. In fall, three
     Landesliga's of each 10 teams play off for 8 spots in the Regional-
     liga West:
     - Landesliga Salzburg (10 teams, 2 to go to Regionalliga West)
     - Landesliga Tirol (10 teams, 3 to go to Regionalliga West)
     - Landesliga Vorarlberg (10 teams, 3 to go to Regionalliga West)
     The winner of the Regionalliga West (in Spring) qualifies for the
     Zweite BL. The teams remaining in the Landesliga's after the fall
     season play a full competition (12 matches in Tirol and Vorarlberg,
     14 in Salzburg) against a number of relegation spots depending on
     what happens in the Zweite BL.

Teams 2-8 will be distributed over the Landesliga's of Salzburg, Tirol
and Vorarlberg in fall 1993, which will play a full round (18 matches)
to find 8 teams for next Spring's Regionalliga West.

Composition of these Landesliga's fall 1993:

Salzburg: ESV Saalfelden, FC Salzburg, USK Anif, FC Zell/See, Grünau,
          Mittersill, Hallwang, Henndorf, Kaprun, ASK Salzburg (Promoted).
          Relegated: SAK 1914.

Tirol: SC Kundl, SV Hall, FC Wacker Amateure, SV Wörgl, Haiming, Fügen,
       Mayrhofen, SK Rum, Axams, FC Vils (Promoted).
       Relegated: none, thanks to Kufstein's promotion.

Vorarlberg: SC Rheindorf Altach, Schwarz-Weiß Bregenz, FC Dornbirn,
            Austria Lustenau, VfB Hohenems, FC Hard, FC Koblach,
            FC Nenzing, FC Schruns, RW Rankwell (Promoted).
            Relegated: FC Wolfurt.

There being no "Regionalliga Mitte", the champions of the Landesliga's
of Steiermark, Kärnten, and Oberösterreich yearly play for one place
in the 2. Bundesliga.
