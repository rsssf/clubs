== 2. Bundesliga 1994/95


<!-- $table3$ - Table -->

Promoted to the 2nd Bundesliga: Favoritner AC (from the
Regionalliga Ost), WSG Wattens (Regionalliga West) and
SAK Klagenfurt (Regionalliga Mitte).

Note: the Spielgemeinschaft between the Wiener SC and SV Gerasdorf was
dissolved at the end of the season, SV Gerasdorf obtaining the place in
the 2. Bundesliga and the Wiener Sport-Club competing in the Ostliga
in 1995/96.

== Feeder Leagues for 2. Liga

--------------------------

<!-- $table4$ - Regionalliga Ost -->

Note: the Spielgemeinschaft between the Wiener SC and SV Gerasdorf was
dissolved at the end of the season, SV Gerasdorf obtaining the place in
the 2. Bundesliga and the Wiener Sport-Club competing in the Ostliga
in 1995/96.

Relegated to the Regionalliga Ost: Stockerau, Wiener Neustadt.
Promoted to the Regionalliga Ost: Baumgarten, Kottingbrunn, Admira/Landhaus.


<!-- $table5$ - Regionalliga Mitte -->

Promoted to the Regionalliga Mitte: Feldkirchen, Eintracht Wels, Hartberg.


<!-- $table6$ - Regionalliga West (Spring season) -->

Relegated to Regionalliga West: see feeder leagues (Salzburg, Tirol,
Vorarlberg); the three feeder leagues first play a fall season before
the eight best teams qualify for the Regionalliga West.

-----------------------------------
